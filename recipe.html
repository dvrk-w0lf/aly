Ingredients:

  • Meats: Beef, pork, poultry (chicken, duck), and game meats like rabbit.
  • Fish: Salmon, sturgeon, herring, and various freshwater fish.
  • Dairy: Sour cream (often used as a condiment), farmer's cheese, and a variety of cheeses.
  • Grains: Wheat, barley, and rye used in bread, cereals, and various dishes.
  • Vegetables: Potatoes, beets, cabbage, onions, carrots, and cucumbers are commonly used.
  • Fruits: Apples, berries (such as lingonberries and cranberries), and plums.
  • Herbs and Spices: Dill, parsley, garlic, bay leaves, and black pepper are frequently used in Russian cuisine.
  • Beverages: Tea is a common drink in Russia, as well as various alcoholic beverages like vodka.

Instructions (A step-by-step guide):

  1. Prepare the beef: Season the beef slices with salt and pepper. In a skillet, heat oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef slices and cook until browned on both sides. Remove the beef from the skillet and set aside.
  2. Saute the onions and mushrooms: In the same skillet, melt butter and sauté the onions until translucent. Add the minced garlic and mushrooms, and cook until the mushrooms are golden and the moisture evaporates.
  3. Make the sauce: Sprinkle flour over the onion and mushroom mixture and stir to combine. Gradually pour in the beef broth while stirring constantly to avoid lumps. Let it simmer until the sauce thickens slightly.
  4. Combine the beef and sauce: Return the beef slices to the skillet with the sauce. Stir in the sour cream, reduce the heat, and let it simmer gently for a few minutes until the beef is cooked through and the flavors meld together. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper if needed.
  5. Serve: Serve the beef stroganoff over cooked egg noodles or rice. Garnish with fresh parsley.
